About EMERGENCY NGO

EMERGENCY is an independent, neutral organisation founded in 1994 to offer free, high-quality medical and surgical care to victims of war, landmines and poverty. In more than 30 years EMERGENCY has treated over 13 million people in 21 countries, building sustainable healthcare facilities, training local personnel and promoting a culture of peace, solidarity and respect for human rights.

The project established in Ukraine is part of a broader humanitarian commitment that also includes projects in Afghanistan, Sudan, Sierra Leone, Italy, Uganda and search-and-rescue missions in the Mediterranean Sea.

For more information about EMERGENCY, please visit the official website.

PHC Project in Ukraine

The Primary Healthcare for Remote Communities in Kharkiv Region, Ukraine project strengthens access to essential health services in the conflict‑affected Kharkiv region and neighbouring rural areas. By rehabilitating or installing outpatient clinics and deploying trained Community Health Workers (CHWs), we bridge the gap between the national health system and isolated villages that have lost clinics, staff and reliable transports.

Since 2024 we have opened a network of 14 clinics and trained multidisciplinary teams that deliver free consultations, medicine refills, health education and referrals for about 13 000 residents, many of whom are elderly, living with chronic diseases or have limited mobility.

The model proved effective during pilot implementation in Oleksandrivka hromada and now informs expansion to Barvinkove hromada, identified through a 2025 needs‑and‑assets assessment as an area with high vulnerability and strong local engagement.

How to Book an Appointment

All residents of Barvinkove and Blyzniuky hromada, including internally displaced people can access EMERGENCY services and receive free of charge care.

Outpatient clinics operate in Barvinkove, Blyzniuky, Oleksandrivka, and Dobrosillia. Mobile clinics cover 30 surrounding villages. CHWs are available for home visits, upon appointment, for people with reduced mobility.

Blood‑pressure and glucose monitoring, prescription renewals, maternal & child health checks, basic laboratory tests, mental‑health first aid and specialist referrals.

To book a home visit ask Gino, our ChatBot!
